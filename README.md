# 📚 StudyMate AI

**Final project for the Building AI course**

---

## Summary

StudyMate AI is a personalised study assistant that helps students plan revision, summarise notes, and generate practice questions using AI. It adapts to each learner’s progress and focuses on improving exam preparation efficiency.

---

## Background

Many students struggle with:
* Organising large amounts of study material
* Knowing what to revise and when
* Creating effective practice questions
* Staying consistent with revision schedules

This problem is extremely common in secondary school, university, and professional exam preparation. I chose this topic because studying efficiently is often more important than studying longer, and many learners don’t have access to personalised tutoring.

This project is important because:
* It reduces stress during exam periods
* It improves learning outcomes
* It makes high-quality study support more accessible

---

## How is it used?

StudyMate AI is used as a web or mobile app.

### Usage process:
1. Student uploads notes (PDF, text, or slides)
2. AI summarises key concepts
3. System generates:
   * flashcards
   * quizzes
   * revision timetable
4. Student tracks progress over time

### Users:
* Secondary school students
* University students
* Adult learners preparing for exams (e.g. language tests, certifications)

The system is used mainly during study sessions and exam preparation periods, especially in the weeks leading up to assessments.

---

## Data sources and AI methods

### Data sources:
* User-uploaded study notes
* Public educational datasets (textbooks, open course material)
* Optional: past exam questions datasets (where legally available)

### AI methods:
* Natural Language Processing (NLP) for summarisation
* Transformer models (e.g. BERT/GPT-style models) for question generation
* Classification models for topic tagging
* Reinforcement learning or feedback loops to improve quiz difficulty over time

---

## Challenges

This project does not solve:
* Guaranteeing correct answers in all generated content
* Understanding highly complex or ambiguous handwritten notes perfectly
* Replacing teachers or formal education

Other challenges:
* Risk of incorrect AI-generated summaries
* Privacy concerns with student data
* Dependence on quality of input notes
* Potential over-reliance on AI instead of active learning

---

## What next?

Future improvements could include:
* Personalised learning “AI tutor” conversations
* Integration with school curricula
* Speech-to-text lecture summarisation
* Group study mode with shared quizzes
* Adaptive difficulty based on performance

With more development, it could become a full AI-powered learning platform used in schools globally.
